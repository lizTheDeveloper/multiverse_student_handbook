## Know Your Rights

### Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA)

**You have the right to:**
- Reasonable accommodations at work
- Accessible public spaces
- Non-discrimination in employment, housing, education
- Service animals
- Accessible websites (for some entities)

**Learn more:**
- **ADA.gov:** [ada.gov](https://www.ada.gov/)
- **ADA National Network:** [adata.org](https://www.adata.org/)
  - 1-800-949-4232
  - Regional ADA centers

### Section 504 & IDEA (For Students)

**Section 504:** Accommodations in school (K-12, college)

**IDEA:** Special education services (K-12)

**Resources:**
- **Understood.org:** [understood.org](https://www.understood.org/)
- **Disability Rights Education & Defense Fund (DREDF):** [dredf.org](https://dredf.org/)

## Disability Benefits

### SSI & SSDI

**SSI (Supplemental Security Income):**
- Need-based
- For people with limited income/resources
- Disability must prevent work

**SSDI (Social Security Disability Insurance):**
- Based on work history
- Must have worked and paid into Social Security

**Apply:**
- [ssa.gov/disability](https://www.ssa.gov/disability/)
- Online, phone, or in-person

**Getting help with application:**
- **Disability Rights organizations** (each state has one)
- **Find yours:** [ndrn.org/about/ndrn-member-agencies](https://www.ndrn.org/about/ndrn-member-agencies/)
- **Lawyers:** Many work on contingency (paid from back pay if you win)

**Know:** First application is often denied. Appeal. Most people win on appeal.

### Other Benefits

**SNAP (Food Stamps)**
- [fns.usda.gov/snap](https://www.fns.usda.gov/snap)

**Medicaid**
- Healthcare for low-income/disabled
- [medicaid.gov](https://www.medicaid.gov/)

- [hud.gov/topics/rental_assistance](https://www.hud.gov/topics/rental_assistance)

**Benefits checkup:**

## Workplace Accommodations

### Job Accommodation Network (JAN)

**Free service helping with:**
- What accommodations to request
- How to request them
- Your rights

**Contact:**
- [askjan.org](https://askjan.org/)
- 1-800-526-7234
- Searchable database by disability

### Common Accommodations

**Physical:**
- Ergonomic furniture
- Wheelchair accessibility
- Adjustable desk/monitor
- Parking accommodation
- Modified schedule

**Sensory:**
- Quiet workspace
- Noise-canceling headphones
- Lighting adjustments
- Sunglasses indoors

**Cognitive/Mental Health:**
- Flexible hours
- Work from home
- Written instructions
- Extended deadlines
- Breaks as needed
- Quiet space for breaks

**Chronic illness/pain:**
- Flexible schedule
- Work from home
- Reduced hours
- Breaks/rest periods
- Modified duties

### Requesting Accommodations

**You don't have to disclose your diagnosis.**

**Instead:**
- "I have a medical condition that requires accommodation"
- "I need [specific accommodation] to perform my job"
- Medical documentation may be required (doctor's note)

**Process:**
1. Request accommodation (preferably in writing)
2. Employer requests medical documentation
3. Interactive process (discuss what works)
4. Accommodation implemented

**If denied unreasonably:**
- **EEOC (Equal Employment Opportunity Commission):** [eeoc.gov](https://www.eeoc.gov/)
- 1-800-669-4000
- File a charge of discrimination

### Spoon Theory

**Coined by Christine Miserandino** to explain chronic illness/pain energy limits.

**Spoons = units of energy.**
- Healthy person: unlimited spoons
- Chronic illness: limited daily spoons
- Every activity costs spoons
- When you're out, you're out

**Learn more:** [butyoudontlooksick.com/articles/written-by-christine/the-spoon-theory](https://butyoudontlooksick.com/articles/written-by-christine/the-spoon-theory/)

## Housing & Accessibility

### Fair Housing Act

**Requires reasonable accommodations** in housing.

**Examples:**
- Service/assistance animals (even if "no pets")
- Accessible parking
- Ramp installation
- Grab bars
- Visual fire alarms

**Contact:**
- **HUD (Housing & Urban Development):** 1-800-669-9777
- [hud.gov/fairhousing](https://www.hud.gov/fairhousing)

## Healthcare Navigation

### Finding Disability-Competent Doctors

**Ask:**
- "What's your experience with [your disability]?"
- "Are you willing to work with me on accommodations?"
- "Do you believe me when I describe my symptoms?"

**Red flags:**
- Dismissive
- "It's all in your head"
- Won't accommodate
- Doesn't listen

**Green flags:**
- Listens
- Takes you seriously
- Willing to collaborate
- Understands your condition

### Advocating for Yourself

**Bring:**
- Written list of symptoms
- Questions
- Support person if helpful

**Say:**
- "I need you to document this in my chart"
- "I need a referral to a specialist"
- "That treatment didn't work, what else can we try?"

**You can:**
- Get a second opinion
- Fire a doctor
- Request a different doctor
- File a complaint

## Multiverse & Disabled Students

**Many of us are disabled.** You belong here.

**Accommodations we can offer:**
- Flexible participation
- Reduced expectations when needed
- Multiple ways to engage (text, voice, async)

**Accommodations we can't offer:**
- 24/7 support
- Professional medical/mental health care
- Crisis intervention

**If you need accommodation, ask.** Facilitators will work with you.
