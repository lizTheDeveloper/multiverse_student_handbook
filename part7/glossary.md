# Glossary of Terms

## A

**Accommodation:** Modifications to environment or expectations that support neurodivergent access without lowering standards.

**Allistic:** Not autistic. Used in neurodivergent communities to describe neurotypical people specifically in relation to autism.

**AASPIRE:** Academic Autism Spectrum Partnership in Research and Education - participatory research organization.

**Autistic Burnout:** Syndrome resulting from chronic life stress and mismatch of expectations/abilities, characterized by exhaustion, loss of function, and reduced stimulus tolerance.

## B

**Boundary Violation:** Behavior that crosses stated limits around time, emotional labor, physical space, or professional role.

## C

**Community-Based Participatory Research (CBPR):** Research approach where community members and academics share power equally.

**Crisis Addiction:** Pattern of creating or amplifying crisis to receive attention and care.

## E

**Executive Function:** Cognitive processes that organize, plan, and execute tasks. Often impacted in ADHD and autism.

## G

**Grandiosity:** Inflated sense of importance or abilities, potentially indicating mania or personality disorder.

## I

**Info-dumping:** Sharing extensive information about special interests, common in autistic communication.

## L

**Love-bombing:** Intense, overwhelming expressions of affection used to create rapid intimacy.

## M

**Masking:** Suppressing neurodivergent traits to appear neurotypical. Exhausting and linked to burnout.

## N

**Neurodivergent:** Having a brain that functions differently from societal norms (ADHD, autism, etc.).

**Neurotypical:** Brain functioning that aligns with societal norms.

## P

**Premature Transcendence:** Attempting to lead or teach before completing necessary healing and skill-building.

## R

**Rejection Sensitive Dysphoria (RSD):** Extreme emotional response to perceived criticism or rejection, common in ADHD.

**Restorative Justice:** Framework focused on repairing relationships after harm.

## S

**Spiritual Bypass:** Using spirituality to avoid accountability or real work.

## T

**Transformative Justice:** Framework that addresses harm AND transforms conditions that created the harm.

**Trauma Bonding:** Unhealthy attachment often formed through intensity, crisis, or perceived rescue.

## U

**Universal Design for Learning (UDL):** Designing educational experiences accessible to all from the start.

---

**Note:** This glossary prioritizes neurodivergent-led definitions and frameworks.
