## The Three New Guides

### 1. Understanding Borderline Personality Disorder (BPD)
**File:** `part7/borderline-personality-disorder.md`
**Length:** ~6,500 words

**Key Content:**
- **BPD as neurodivergence** (recent framework shift)
- **Critical overlap with autism/ADHD** (60% with BPD had childhood ADHD; commonly misdiagnosed)
- **Four concerning patterns in educational settings:**
  1. Idealization → Devaluation cycles
  2. Crisis escalation when needs aren't met
  3. Boundary testing & push-pull dynamics
  4. Identity instability & over-attachment to community
- **Distinguishing BPD from:**
  - RSD (Rejection Sensitive Dysphoria - ADHD)
  - Autistic burnout/trauma
  - C-PTSD
- **Evidence-based treatment:** DBT (Dialectical Behavior Therapy)
- **Teacher scripts** for each pattern
- **Disability justice lens** on reducing stigma
- **Self-care for teachers** working with BPD patterns

**Research Grounded In:**
- 2023 systematic review: "Comorbidity and Overlaps between Autism Spectrum and BPD"
- Neurodivergent Insights research on ADHD/BPD overlap
- Disability justice perspectives on BPD
- Therapist Neurodiversity Collective frameworks

**Key Principle:** "Consistent, boundaried care—not rescue—helps students with BPD thrive."

### 2. Understanding Schizophrenia & Psychosis
**File:** `part7/schizophrenia-psychosis.md`
**Length:** ~6,800 words

**Key Content:**
- **Critical statistics:** 82% of college students with first episode psychosis take leave; schizophrenia is #1 reason students drop out of high school
- **Recognizing early warning signs** (prodromal phase)
- **Five patterns of active psychosis:**
  1. Disorganized communication
  2. Paranoid/persecutory delusions
  3. Grandiose delusions
  4. Hallucinations
  5. Disorganized/catatonic behavior
- **How to respond:** Stay calm, don't argue with delusions, assess safety, refer to crisis services
- **Special considerations:**
  - Substance-induced psychosis
  - Spiritual emergency vs. psychosis
  - Autism, trauma, and psychosis
- **Supporting students in recovery** (accommodations, expectations)
- **Evidence-based treatment:** Coordinated Specialty Care (CSC) programs
- **Dismantling stigma** (myth-busting about violence, functionality)

**Research Grounded In:**
- ISPS-US (International Society for Psychological and Social Approaches to Psychosis)
- Students With Psychosis (SWP) - peer-led organization
- NAMI 2025 whitepaper on Coordinated Specialty Care
- Yale Medicine research on supporting students with psychosis
- Strong 365 - first episode psychosis support

**Key Principle:** "Early intervention saves lives—recognize and refer quickly."

### 3. Understanding Dependent Personality Disorder (DPD)
**File:** `part7/dependent-personality-disorder.md`
**Length:** ~5,200 words

**Key Content:**
- **Distinguishing DPD from neurodivergent support needs** (critical for ADHD students)
- **Five concerning patterns:**
  1. Excessive reassurance-seeking
  2. Inability to disagree or set boundaries
  3. Crisis when support is unavailable
  4. Over-reliance on teacher for life decisions
  5. Urgently seeking new "caretaker" figures
- **Distinguishing DPD from:**
  - Anxious attachment (trauma response)
  - ADHD executive function needs
  - Learned helplessness
- **Building autonomy:** Gradual independence scaffolding (weeks 1-2: high structure → weeks 5+: low structure)
- **Evidence-based treatment:** CBT, assertiveness training, family-focused therapy
- **Teacher scripts** for each pattern
- **Self-care:** Why this work feels draining and what helps

**Research Grounded In:**
- StatPearls/NCBI clinical research on DPD
- Family-focused therapy approaches
- Treatment models emphasizing autonomy-building
- Community mental health resources (Indiana Center for Recovery, HelpGuide.org)

**Key Principle:** "Scaffolded independence, not rescue. Your boundaries help them grow."

## Common Threads Across All Three Guides

### 1. Non-Stigmatizing, Neurodivergent-Affirming
- Person-first and identity-first language
- Recognition that many people with these conditions function well
- Clear about when it IS a problem vs. when it's not
- Disability justice lens throughout

### 2. Recognition, Not Diagnosis
- Each guide emphasizes: **You are an educator, not a clinician**
- Focus on observable patterns in educational settings
- Clear distinction between one-time behavior and patterns
- When to escalate to professionals

### 3. Practical & Actionable
- Specific scripts for teachers to use
- Clear "What to do" sections for each pattern
- Decision trees (when is it a problem? when is it not?)
- Self-care guidance for teachers

### 4. Research-Backed
- All three cite current research and evidence-based practices
- Organizations and resources listed at the end of each guide
- Grounded in peer-led and professional frameworks

### 5. Overlap with Neurodivergence
- **BPD:** High comorbidity with ADHD/autism; often misdiagnosed
- **Psychosis:** Some autistic traits can be mistaken for psychosis
- **DPD:** Easily confused with ADHD executive function needs

### 6. Context Over Judgment
- Explains WHY patterns happen (trauma, brain differences, learned responses)
- Separates the person from the behavior
- Compassion + boundaries held together

## What Makes These Different from Typical Mental Health Resources

### 1. Educational Context-Specific
Most mental health resources are for:
- Clinicians (diagnosis and treatment)
- Family members (living with someone with the condition)
- General public (awareness)

**These guides are for educators** managing classroom/community dynamics while maintaining appropriate boundaries.

### 2. Neurodivergent-Affirming
Most resources pathologize. These guides:
- Recognize BPD as neurodivergence
- Note high misdiagnosis rates in autistic/ADHD people
- Distinguish communication style from pathology
- Use disability justice frameworks

### 3. Practical Pattern Recognition
Rather than DSM criteria, these focus on:
- How does this actually show up in a Discord chat?
- What does this look like in online learning?
- What do I say when this happens?
- When is this my problem vs. a clinical problem?

### 4. Self-Care Integrated
Each guide includes a section on why this work is hard on teachers and what helps.
